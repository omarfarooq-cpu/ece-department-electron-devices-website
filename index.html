Course Description

Electron Devices is a foundational course that introduces semiconductor physics, diode theory, transistor operation, field effect devices, power control components, and optoelectronic devices. This course builds the fundamental knowledge required for analog electronics, digital systems, VLSI, and power electronics.

Total Credits

  • L: 3
  • T: 0
  • P: 0
  • C: 3
  • Total Hours: 45

Course Objectives

  • Understand construction and characteristics of PN junction diodes.
  • Analyze operation and biasing of BJT.
  • Study JFET and MOSFET characteristics.
  • Learn SCR and TRIAC working principles.
  • Explore special diodes and optoelectronic devices.

Prerequisites

  • Fundamentals of Physics
  • Basic Electrical Circuits

Course Outcome

  • Understand semiconductor device physics
  • Analyze electronic device characteristics
  • Design basic analog circuits
  • Apply power devices in real-world applications
